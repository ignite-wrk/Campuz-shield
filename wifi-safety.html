Public Wi-Fi Safety Tips for Students

Posted on: March 5, 2024 | Category: Wi-Fi Safety

Public Wi-Fi networks, such as those found in cafes, libraries, or campus hotspots, are convenient but often insecure. Cybercriminals can easily intercept data transmitted over open networks, putting your personal information at risk.

  • Data Interception: Attackers can use sniffing tools to capture login details and messages.
  • Fake Hotspots: Hackers can create look-alike Wi-Fi names to trick students into connecting.
  • Session Hijacking: Active logins (like email or student portals) can be stolen during transmission.

  1. Always use a VPN (Virtual Private Network) to encrypt your internet traffic.
  2. Avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts when using public Wi-Fi.
  3. Keep your operating system and antivirus software updated.
  4. Use your mobile hotspot instead of open networks for important tasks.
  5. Disable automatic Wi-Fi connection on your phone or laptop.
